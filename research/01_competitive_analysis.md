# Speakerly — Competitive Analysis of AAC Apps

## What is AAC?

**Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC)** encompasses tools and strategies used by people who cannot rely on natural speech. These range from low-tech picture boards to high-tech speech-generating apps on tablets. The market is dominated by a handful of expensive, symbol-grid-based apps — and is ripe for AI-driven disruption.

## Common User Complaints (from Reddit & reviews)

### 💰 Price Barrier
- Apps cost **$150–$300** — prohibitive for many families
- "Crazy expensive" is the most common sentiment
- Subscription models create ongoing financial burden
- Schools/institutions often can't afford bulk licenses

### 🤖 Robotic, Impersonal Voices
- Generic TTS voices feel dehumanizing
- Users want voices that sound like *them*, not a robot
- Limited personalization of voice tone, pace, personality

### 📱 Platform Lock-in
- Most top apps are **iOS-only** — forces expensive iPad purchase
- No easy way to move between devices
- Limited phone-sized interfaces for portability

### ⏱️ Slow Communication
- Building sentences word-by-word is painfully slow
- By the time a message is assembled, the conversation has moved on
- No contextual awareness — same buttons whether ordering coffee or at a hospital
- **"The biggest frustration is speed — conversations feel like they're happening TO you, not WITH you"**

### 🧩 Setup Complexity
- Configuring grids and vocabulary is overwhelming
- Requires speech therapist involvement for initial setup
- Parents/caregivers need extensive training

### 🎯 Not Adaptive
- Same interface regardless of context, time of day, or who you're talking to
- No learning from usage patterns (in most apps)
- Vocabulary often doesn't grow with the user

## Gap Analysis: Where Speakerly Can Win

| Current State | Speakerly Opportunity |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Word-by-word sentence building | **AI predicts entire sentences and responses** based on context |
| Same interface everywhere | **Context-adaptive UI** — coffee shop vs. hospital vs. home |
| No personality | **Personality profile** — humor, formality, warmth, directness |
| Generic robot voice | **Personalized voice cloning** (leverage Apple Personal Voice / ElevenLabs) |
| Expensive ($150–$300) | **Freemium or affordable subscription** model |
| iOS-only typically | **Web-first → native apps** for maximum reach |
| Static vocabulary | **Learning system** that improves with every conversation |
| No relationship awareness | **Person-specific conversation history** — knows how you talk to mom vs. your doctor |
| Complex setup | **AI-guided onboarding** that builds your profile through conversation |
| No contextual awareness | **Location, time, calendar, social context** all feed into predictions |

---

## Key Insight

> **Every existing AAC app treats communication as a mechanical task — pressing buttons to assemble words.** Speakerly should treat communication as a **collaborative, intelligent, contextual experience** where AI handles the heavy lifting and the user simply confirms or adjusts. The goal is to make communication feel as close to natural conversation speed as possible.

## Market Size & Opportunity

- ~2 million AAC users in the US alone; ~100 million worldwide need some form of AAC
- AAC market size estimated at **$3.2B+ globally** (growing ~7% annually)
- Current apps serve primarily children with developmental disabilities
- **Massively underserved**: adults with ALS, stroke, Parkinson's, brain injury, laryngectomy
- AI-native AAC is still in its infancy — **first-mover advantage is available now**
